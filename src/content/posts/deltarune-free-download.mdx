## Plot

The main character is Kris, a mischievous boy who, together with his companion Susie, travels through the Dark World. He discovers a passage to this world in the school closet and must restore the balance between light and darkness there.

## Gameplay

In terms of gameplay, Deltarune differs from its predecessor in several ways. While in Undertale we controlled a single character and observed battles from a first-person perspective — not to mention the arcade elements — here we are dealing with a system more reminiscent of classic JRPGs.

The combat system in the successor to Undertale has developed in a rather unexpected direction. Rather than controlling a single character, we now control a whole three-person team. Each of them can perform more than the four actions we saw in the original game. Initially, we have the ability to attack, defend against attacks, use spells, show mercy, and so on.

## Try to ensure that all the monsters survive

It is important to show mercy because we should not kill monsters. However, this is not so easy, especially since Susie at the beginning can launch attacks without our interference.

The arcade theme of avoiding enemy attacks in a manner similar to bullet hell games, as seen in the previous game, is back in full force. Dodging attacks 'by a hair's breadth' is an added bonus. When it comes to attacking opponents, there is a mini-game for reflexes which determines how much damage is inflicted.

The most important thing, however, is what was explained at the beginning of both games: no one here has to die. Every enemy can be saved from their own stupidity (i.e. attacking our team) or from a bloodthirsty lady with an axe.
