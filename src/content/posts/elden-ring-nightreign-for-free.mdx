## First and foremost, the focus is on cooperation

The gameplay involves three-person trips to Limveld, a specific version of the Lands Between from Elden Ring. There, we must survive for two days and two nights in order to face one of the eight Lords of the Night — and that's not all of the main bosses!

Before setting off from the Roundtable Hold, where we can check out different weapons, choose new skins, read the journal and so on, we select one of the eight characters and relics, which give us passive bonuses. We unlock these after each session.

Then, during about 30 minutes of basic gameplay, we run very fast, climb very high, jump and sometimes even fly, trying to prepare ourselves as best we can for the final battle.

## What's the best way to develop your character? Combat!

Along the way, you have to kill rank-and-file enemies and bosses scattered around the board (some of whom are old friends from Dark Souls 1, 2 and 3!), for which you gain experience that allows you to level up. This happens automatically when you click a button on the controller/keyboard at a Site of Grace.

The power of our character is most affected by weapons. We always start with basic weapons, but it is advisable to quickly rearm by collecting items from chests or from the most powerful opponents during the session.

In addition, visiting church ruins is mandatory if you want more health potions, and it is sometimes worth going to caves to look for stones that can be used to upgrade weapons or provide rare passive enhancements, as these can significantly alter your build. Although the map is partially randomly generated, we usually end up in the same places.

## Let yourself be surprised!

Over time, however, our world will become haunted by unique cataclysms and events. I remember being shocked when a huge crater suddenly appeared in the middle of the map, the ground began to shake and a huge ball of lava rose up!

The developers have prepared a total of four such events, and believe me, it's worth discovering their secrets for the useful bonuses alone. But that's not all: sometimes we will be cursed and invaded, and we will have to fight our clones.

## Nightreign also has an interesting storyline

Not enough? Then let's add the entire story setting. It's presented differently to what we're used to from FromSoftware. Here, we won't be reading between the lines, relying on item descriptions and NPC mutterings. Each character has their own storyline, complete with plot twists and endings.

These stories are unlocked through a memory system that requires us to complete specific tasks. Find this, kill that, talk to the other. Speaking of characters, we start with six, but we'll quickly unlock the other two.

My favourites are: Iron Eye, the archer, and Rekluza, the sorceress. Iron Eye is very easy to play and is essential for defeating most of the Lords of the Night thanks to his attack range. Rekluza, on the other hand, requires a lot of skill, but is great fun thanks to the various spell combinations available.

## The graphics and music were high quality

The game ran really smoothly. Although the engine is not particularly impressive, the artists have managed to create a lot of beauty. Random events on the map can be visually stunning, and some of the battles with the Lords of the Night are spectacular to behold from a safe distance. The soundtrack also enhances the gameplay, as is usually the case with FromSoft.

The enormous amount of work that went into Nightreign is evident; it's not just a simple overlay or a cash grab. It's a full-fledged game that, once you know what to do and have a good team by your side, provides a great deal of satisfaction, particularly when it comes to devising and executing tactics.
