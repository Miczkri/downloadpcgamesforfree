## Instead of a hero, a man from nowhere

When a game gives you a ready-made hero, the story follows a predetermined path. Octopath Traveler 0 does not follow the usual pattern. Instead, players find themselves in the ruins of Wishvale and must do something about it. Alone. Your character, created from scratch, is not special at first. At least at first.

In the game, you can choose your character's appearance, voice, gender, and even favorite food. These are not just cosmetic changes; the developers at Square Enix emphasize that these elements impact the behavior of non-player characters and the development of dialogue. Furthermore, it is your decisions that will bring the city back to life. Once full of people, Wishvale now resembles a burnt-out crater. It's up to you who will return and how the city will change over time.

However, reconstruction is different from the crafting or resource management found in other games. It's more about people than resources — you must find people to help rebuild the community. Sometimes you have to convince them with words, sometimes with fighting, and sometimes with something completely different.

## Fight alongside eight companions and explore even more paths!

The combat mechanics are still based on the familiar Break & Boost system, but this time, a more complete range of options is available. First, your team can have up to eight people, which significantly affects your tactics. With more characters comes more variables, making it more difficult to strike a balance between offense and support.

Recruitment is also an important part of the game. The developers have announced over thirty potential allies, each with their own story, character, and fighting style. Each has their own story, character, and fighting style. But that's not all. Some will be useful not on the battlefield but in Wishvale itself. Sometimes, you will have to take a risk and trust an uncertain person, which could lead to unexpected benefits or get you into trouble.

The Path Actions system, i.e., interactions with NPCs you encounter, will also return. Depending on your character, you can talk, bribe, manipulate, or use force. This opens the way to different endings and a nonlinear narrative. One world, but many ways to experience it.

## Turn-based combat

Speaking of combat, battles are turn-based. During them, you take control of eight characters arranged in two rows on the battlefield. The game allows us to switch between the first and second rows, and the key to success is the skillful use of the Break & Boost system.

After reducing the enemy's shield to zero, we can "break" their defense and launch critical attacks. After charging the Boost Point (BP) meter, we can significantly increase the power of our attacks and improve the effectiveness of support and regeneration abilities.

## Is Octopath Traveler 0 still the same series?

For many players, this may be the key question. Although the world, graphics (still in the characteristic HD-2D style), and some locations are familiar, the approach to storytelling is completely different. Rather than intertwining storylines of eight characters, players get to know one character from scratch. Instead of a ready-made world, there is a ruined town that needs to be rebuilt. However, it is precisely this change that holds potential. The game does not abandon its roots but rather develops them in a new direction.

Interestingly, even though it's a prequel, fans of the previous installments will find subtle connections to events from Octopath Traveler and Octopath Traveler II. Will these connections impact the future of the series? That remains to be seen. But one thing is certain: Square Enix is not afraid to experiment.
