## Unity is a new, groundbreaking game engine

It is also worth noting that, in recent years, the producers have gone further by creating console versions of Football Manager. Previously, titles in this series were only available on PCs. This has recently changed, and the latest instalment will be playable on the PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X, for example.

However, the most important thing is the changes. Football Manager 2026 is the first instalment in the series to run on the Unity engine. This technological change means not only a new look, but also greater opportunities for future development. This is already evident in the completely redesigned interface. It is now easier to navigate the flood of information, and decision-making — whether concerning the line-up, transfers, or tactics — is intended to be more intuitive.

Despite the switch to a new engine, FM26 will support saved games from Football Manager 2024 and 2023 on PC, consoles, and the Nintendo Switch. This is good news for those who want to continue their careers without starting from scratch. Importantly, for the first time, the mobile version will also be able to transfer saved games between editions.

## Premier League licence

However, the biggest news is that FM26 has secured a full Premier League licence. This includes the official logos, kits and team names, as well as realistic match settings inspired by real broadcasts.

Thanks to this, every match in the English Premier League will look and sound exactly like the real thing. Arsenal, Liverpool and Manchester City fans will be able to experience the stadium atmosphere even before the players emerge from the tunnel. Players have been waiting years for this step, which brings the game closer than ever to real football.

## New gameplay features

The matchday mode has also been expanded. You can see more details on the pitch, and the new animation system makes the players' movements look more natural. The developers are calling it the most spectacular FM yet. The first video previews look promising.

Alongside major changes, the developers have announced a number of smaller improvements. These include transfers, tactics, manager creation and integration with console controllers.

Changes have also been made to the artificial intelligence 'under the hood'. Rival managers are said to react better to the situation on the pitch, and the transfer system has new algorithms that are said to make clubs' moves more realistic. Taken together, all these changes make FM26 look set to be the most ambitious instalment in the series' history.

For the first time in history, Football Manager 26 will feature the ability to manage women's club and national teams. The developers have announced that this will be an integral part of the game, rather than a separate mode or add-on.

In practice, this means new tactical challenges and a different style of play, as well as an opportunity to familiarise yourself with the rapidly developing women's football scene. It is a response to the growing interest in women's competitions, and further proof that Sports Interactive wants Football Manager to reflect the full richness of modern football.

FM26 is set to show that patience pays off. The developers emphasise that the game is not a revolution for the sake of it. Every new feature has been introduced with quality and realism in mind. If the announcements prove true, we could be looking at one of the highest-rated instalments in the series' history.
