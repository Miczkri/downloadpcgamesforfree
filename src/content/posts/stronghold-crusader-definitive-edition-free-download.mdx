The previous version, available on Steam or GOG, offers plenty of content. It includes 80 scenarios in two pseudo-campaigns and a dozen missions based on historical battles. It also has a great template-based map generator and map editor, as well as multiplayer.

The package also includes the Extreme Edition, which features hardcore scenarios and boards that allow battles with thousands of units. So why the remaster?

## More and prettier!

First of all, Stronghold Crusader: Definitive Edition comes to the rescue, primarily in terms of graphics. The old graphics are still charming, but once you see the refreshed textures, you won't be able to comfortably go back to the original.

Second, it aims to improve the multiplayer experience with a standard matchmaking system and a convenient option to play with friends instead of outdated solutions or antiquated external programs, such as Hamachi.

The third point seems to be the most important. Rarely does the release of old games add anything more than maps. Firefly has gone above and beyond, though, because the game will be enriched with eight new units, including a Bedouin scout, a camel lancer, an armored camel, and a wall breaker.

## New and improved units and lords

As we know, the Bedouins were well adapted to life in the desert. The developers of the revamped Crusader emphasized this with their equipment. We have skilled sappers, troops who specialize in setting traps, eunuchs who wield two scimitars each, and irreplaceable healers on the battlefield.

However, Stronghold Crusader: Definitive Edition has to offer. Firefly has also introduced new AI-controlled enemies. The developers emphasize that each character has been designed to embody the military and cultural aspects of the ancient world properly.

## Create your own missions

The game's roadmap includes two main categories of new content: free content released as part of standard updates and paid content. The first category includes a new economic campaign with five missions centered on disrupted water trade routes on the Nile, which will be available in the fall. There will also be a new ruler with whom we can compete.

In the winter, a series of ten missions for cooperative mode will debut, along with the ability to modify castles, rulers, and campaigns. Special tools will be created for this purpose that will also allow you to design rulers and their strongholds from scratch for your own hand-designed missions.

## A few minor changes

As for the familiar elements of the game, the most notable change is the significantly altered appearance of the knights and soldiers. Their tunics and horse caparisons are now less white, and the crosses no longer span the entire height and width of the shields or horse coverings.

Firefly Studios wouldn't be Firefly Studios if they didn't boast about the game's popularity on Steam. As many as 150,000 players added the Crusader re-release to their wish lists, and 130,000 downloaded the demo. These are great results for a remaster of an almost 25-year-old RTS game.

Stronghold Crusader: Definitive Edition will debut on PC on July 15.
