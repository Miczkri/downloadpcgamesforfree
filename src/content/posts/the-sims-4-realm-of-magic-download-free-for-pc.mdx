## Wizard by choice!

As is usually the case with The Sims expansions, the new elements are visible from the character creation stage. Although relatively few clothing items have been added, the expansion enables you to create a character who is part human and part wizard, and who possesses a stone that enables them to travel to the land of magic.

Of course, nothing prevents existing or new, more 'human' characters from entering the world of secret powers. The newly added neighbourhood features an atmospheric bar, residences and a mysterious portal that transports Sims to the land of magic. This land is teeming with masters of sorcery, magic cauldrons, spell books and stalls selling all kinds of items. Does this remind you of anything?

Interestingly, this is where a very nice feature of the expansion pack comes into play: many of the new objects can be used to practise magic. However, Sims who are not naturally wizards or who have not undergone a rite of passage will not see the secret options.

For instance, an average character will use a cauldron for brewing potions to cook soup at best. Similarly, in the mystical library, ordinary characters will read novels about the adventures of young wizards instead of discovering new spells.

## Great power and endless possibilities

The Sims 4 Realm of Magic expansion pack offers a wide range of interesting supernatural features. One of the first spells I learnt was how to conjure food out of thin air. This is great, especially for those who aren't the best at cooking.

And that's just the start of a wide range of new abilities. I quickly learned how to brew potions that could change a character's mood, provide various bonuses or even harm other Sims. After an intense spellcasting session, however, I disturbed the balance of magic and a curse fell upon me.

From that moment on, everyone who met my character instantly hated her. Before I found the antidote, I got into two fights. The outcomes could have been different, but this adventure was an exciting and interesting surprise.

## Abracadabra is all about spells and magic

I won't describe all the spells here, because discovering them yourself is part of the fun. Sometimes, however, I got the impression that the creators lacked ideas and that magic was mainly used to make the game easier. The same applies to character upgrades when we collect the right amount of aspiration and whim points.

I must also admit that, at times, reaching new levels of magic and learning could be tedious. All the fun is limited to repetitive activities. Experience points are gained by experimenting with the cauldron, reading books or discussing spells with other adepts of the occult arts. These activities must be repeated over many Sim days to reach maximum magical potential.

## Is this expansion worth playing?

The concept of magic is popular among readers of books, viewers of films and players of games. For this reason alone, Realm of Magic will certainly appeal to anyone looking to enhance their experience of the basic version of the game. This additional pack is a good choice because it significantly increases a character's abilities and allows for interesting development.

All of these useful and amusing effects can be unlocked over the course of a few enjoyable evenings of gameplay. So, if you feel that the 'life simulator' lacks madness and otherworldly action, why not give it a try? It's a great alternative to Harry Potter in the Sims universe.
