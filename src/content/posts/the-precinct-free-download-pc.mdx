## What is The Precinct about?

Behind every production, regardless of its budget, is some kind of download with references to competing works and pop culture. In this respect, The Precinct takes its cue from thriller cinema in a noir climate. It tells the story of fresh-faced police officer Nick Cordell Jr, who is highly motivated to fight crime.

The youngster is said to have his hands full in his hometown of Avernum at a time when street gangs were in vogue. Our crusade will begin in 1983, after the main character has left the police academy to start changing the world for the better. His uniform, however, will "weigh" more than that of the average cadet, as he also joins the police force for personal reasons. While hunting down robbers and murderers, he wants to solve the mysterious death of his father.

Before casting our nets for the big investigation, we go through a series of minor cases involving unpaid parking fines, fights on the pavement, beatings and theft of goods. This is not the routine work of a policeman, but is intended to help us gain experience and confidence in independent operations. After a short mission, we are immediately thrown into the lion's mouth, represented by gangsters, social outcasts and people willing to sell their own mother.

## The gameplay in The Presinct feels almost like a cop simulator

What should interest sandbox fans is the cross-section of activities in the game. The reality of a policeman's work will be well integrated into the gameplay, and the developers will illustrate the daily duties and dangers of the job.

When The Precinct launches, we will be able to issue fines, check parking meters, patrol the streets on foot, tow cars, search for suspects, chase criminals and deal with them using firearms and hand-to-hand combat - leading them to arrest or, as a last resort, to death.

It is said that missions will never repeat during the game, which is guaranteed by procedurally generated crime scenarios. It's worth noting that the game involves shooting and using cover, as well as ambushing hostages.

## Police activities

Separately, the theme of pursuits should be developed. The game implements a menu of options to support the pursuit of criminals, consisting of the use of a turnpike, summoning support, blocking the road and pursuit from a helicopter.

To use these, you need tokens, which you earn by staying close to the fugitive. Each action is tightly coupled with a collision system to make the gameplay impressive and as realistic as possible.

Working in the police station will be an important part of the simulation. Not only by exploring the office area or the prison, but also by taking part in briefings and escorting prisoners.

## Old school GTA atmosphere mixed with seriousness

The open world, the nature of the gameplay and the confrontation between the forces of "good and evil" clearly show the similarities to the world's most popular game series. The release of The Precinct will be accompanied by comments comparing the game to a GTA clone, but with references to older installments that Rockstar Games had not originally set its hand to.

The game's isometric camera, in particular, evokes an old-school vibe, while the dark atmosphere also evokes memories of the long-forgotten GTA II, which was unique in the series for its bleak setting.

Of course, there are many more related elements. We have familiar solutions from the interface, such as a circular radar with a section of the map on the left, mission markers.

We will also notice a system of destruction of the environment, freedom to explore the city and, despite the police environment, the possibility of committing misdemeanours, only in the name of justice - it is not fully said whether we can kill passers-by and devastate the city with impunity, and possibly what penalties await for deviations from morality.

If you were to sum up the main features of The Precinct in a nutshell, it could be argued that this is exactly what Grand Theft Auto would look like today, firstly in terms of the TPP camera mode, then in terms of the game mechanics, and finally in terms of the reversal of the climate from gangster parody to a serious depiction of police work.
