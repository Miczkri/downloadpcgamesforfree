## What's new in NBA 2K26?

This year's NBA 2K26 focuses on greater realism in both the game and the presentation of the entire spectacle. Players have more detailed models thanks to new shading technology, and the courts have refreshed lighting — you can really see the difference.

There have also been many changes to the commentary. Alongside the familiar voices, new analysts, reporters, and announcers have been added to bring more energy and diversity to the broadcast. Commentary at the end of games has also changed to better reflect the tension and drama of key moments. (I recommend throwing a three-pointer in the final seconds!)

The most important new feature when it comes to gameplay is the ProPLAY Motion Engine. Based on real NBA game footage, this solution makes the players' movements look and behave more naturally than in previous editions. Footwork, acceleration, and stopping dynamics have been improved, as has the shooting system itself. These changes introduce a more intuitive pace and greater control over every move. The range of offensive and defensive tools has also been expanded. New layup variants, additional post-moves, and the ability to play defense more precisely give you more room to maneuver in the game. Your opponents' AI has been improved, and the whole game has been sped up, requiring faster decisions and better reactions from players. It does make a difference, although mainly in the details.

## My impressions of NBA 2K26

The first thing that caught my eye in the reviewed NBA 2K26 was the movement of the players and the AI. The players run more smoothly and react faster to commands. There is no longer a sluggish feeling on defense. On offense, dynamic drives to the basket are now much more satisfying. A new shooting system has been added, which is a small change but one that is immediately noticeable. I liked the fill-up arrow, but the new indicator is even better!

It's worth taking a moment to explore MyPlayer mode, which looks solid this year. The Called Out of Bounds campaign once again focuses on the MyPlayer character's path to the NBA, and our performances and choices play a significant role. We start as an anonymous high school player, and every game we play — whether with a youth team or a European club — affects our future in the NBA. This brings back the emotional weight of the original MyPlayer mode (then called MyCareer), when you had to prove your worth step by step. A huge advantage!

It's also difficult to overlook the new animations and the accurate reproduction of NBA stars' movements. Take, for example, Thompson's signature three-point shot, Jokic's "muddy" layups, and Durant's graceful mid-range shot. These details demonstrate the level of work put into creating an immersive experience. Details like these make the game look and feel more authentic. Clearly, ProPLAY isn't just a marketing slogan; it actually affects the gameplay experience, which is always worth appreciating.

Of course, not everything went perfectly, and a few elements still need refinement — I'll discuss those in a moment. Still, my first few hours with NBA 2K26 made it clear that the series is taking another step toward more dynamic and realistic basketball. There's no revolution, but I don't think anyone was expecting one.
