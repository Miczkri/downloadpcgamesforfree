## Familiar framework

Killing Floor 3 is a cooperative shooter in which groups of up to six players take on the roles of Nightfall representatives. Their task is to stop the monstrous zombie army of the Horzine megacorporation.

The action takes place in 2091, at a time when humans have almost been completely dominated by beasts. The premise of the story is not particularly complicated, but then again, no one is going to play this title for the story.

During the presentation, the developers mentioned that Killing Floor 3 can also be easily approached by solo players, but, to be honest, I can't see that happening. The gameplay is entirely focused on cooperation because we can only count on a helping hand in such circumstances – truth be told, I love shooting all kinds of hordes with my friends while chatting about everyday life.

## Get ready to cooperate!

During testing, we exchanged comments, supported each other with advice or simply asked the developers for help and tips on how to survive the next round. Killing Floor 3 offers the most fun in this kind of gameplay, and to be honest, I wouldn't play it without proper support, even if it's from strangers.

At first glance, the gameplay in Killing Floor 3 does not differ significantly from that of many other games in the genre. A group of players arrive at a location and perform simple, repetitive missions consisting of the classic 'go and click', all the while fending off successive waves of enemies.

Tripwire Interactive's formula hasn't changed much, but this system still works, so I'm not surprised that the developers decided not to make any major changes.

## The series is heading in the right direction

However, the developers have not simply cashed in on the series' popularity, as Killing Floor 3 focuses on much greater progression. Each of the six specialisations can reach 30 levels, using points in three skill trees. Players can swap gadgets and develop their abilities to create increasingly complex and powerful combinations.

During testing, I had the opportunity to try out several characters, and I found that their skills could really influence the success of a mission.

## Choose your character class!

Players can take on the role of a sniper, ninja, medic, engineer, arsonist or commando. Each character offers a slightly different experience – it's easy to see which specialisations are geared towards helping the team, but in Killing Floor 3, everyone really has to attack. There is no time or place for calm gameplay in this game.

Customisation is not limited to characters. The armoury plays a key role in Killing Floor 3, as players can create over 150 modifications for various weapons.

Fortunately, the developers have not blocked access to weapons again, so every specialist can visit the store and purchase a variety of equipment. However, players who collect more resources and unlock blueprints gain access to an extensive range of items.

## Killing Floor 3 is worth playing

Tripwire Interactive isn't reinventing the wheel. The developers have built on a well-received experience from a previous game. If you enjoyed Killing Floor 2, you will have a lot of fun playing its sequel.

Killing Floor 3 does not introduce revolutionary changes to the systems, nor does it break new ground for the genre as a whole. Instead, it is a calm evolution of existing mechanics designed to appeal to a loyal audience. The game is great fun, and with the right team by your side, you'll really enjoy it.
