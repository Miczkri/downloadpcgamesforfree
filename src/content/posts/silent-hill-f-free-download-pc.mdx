## Ebisugaoka: the dark secrets of mountainous Japan

Silent Hill: F is set in the mysterious town of Ebisugaoka, Japan's answer to the classic Silent Hill. Inspired by the real Kanayama in Gifu Prefecture, it has been translated into a setting of horror and supernatural events. Ebisugaoka is a small mountain town where tradition intertwines with the everyday problems of its inhabitants. The apparent tranquillity is suddenly and brutally disrupted by the appearance of a mysterious, thick fog that seems to engulf reality.

The main character, Hinako Shimizu, is a teenager trying to find her place in the world. Her life is turned upside down when Ebisugaoka begins to reveal its dark side. As they explore the streets and buildings, players must solve complex puzzles, face terrifying monsters and uncover fragments of history that explain the source of the mystery. This intense narrative, combined with beautifully designed spaces, makes every minute spent in Ebisugaoka tense and engaging, reminiscent of the horror genre's greatest classics.

## An innovative approach to gameplay!

Silent Hill F introduces significant changes to the game mechanics, setting it apart from previous instalments in the series. While classic Silent Hill games focused on exploration, puzzle-solving and survival with limited resources, this game emphasises a more dynamic and demanding approach to hand-to-hand combat. As Hinako is not a natural fighter, the player must improvise by using everyday items, ranging from simple pipes to spears, to take on a variety of grotesque opponents.

This not only increases the sense of danger, but also puts players' creativity and reflexes to the test, offering an interesting change for fans of the series. Additionally, the game features extensive environmental puzzles that require players to analyse their surroundings thoroughly and understand Japanese symbolism and culture. This combination makes Silent Hill: F not only an action game, but also a profound intellectual experience that challenges reflexes and logical thinking skills alike.

## Harmony of beauty and horror

In Silent Hill F, the developers have focused on a unique combination of aesthetics and sound to create the game's distinctive atmosphere. Thanks to the use of Unreal Engine 5, every part of Ebisugaoka is rendered with extraordinary attention to detail — from swaying leaves and realistic lighting to dynamic fog that almost becomes a character in itself. The monster designs draw inspiration from traditional Japanese legends while offering a modern artistic style that can send shivers down the spine of even the most resilient player.

Once again, the sound design is the work of Akira Yamaoka and Kensuke Inage. They have created a soundtrack that balances subtle tension with sudden, intense moments of horror. This selection of music and sound effects perfectly emphasises the game's psychological dimension, putting players in a constant state of anxiety and anticipation as they wait for something terrifying to emerge from the fog.

Silent Hill F's script was written by Ryukishi07, creator of the extremely complex and dark Higurashi: When They Cry series. Thanks to his expertise, the game is not just a simple horror story, but a complex exploration of human fears, social pressures, loneliness and the boundaries between reality and illusion. The story of Hinako and the town of Ebisugaoka is full of symbolism, with red spider lilies playing an important role — flowers that symbolise life, death, and rebirth. Through its narrative, Silent Hill F encourages players to reflect on fear, memory, and the darkest recesses of the human psyche.
