This android is also a fugitive, wanted by his former employers. However, fate has led him to a completely different place than the protagonist of the first game. The protagonist ends up in the Starward Belt, at the very edge of the Helion system, with a stolen ship in a deplorable state, an equally damaged robotic body and amnesia. ## Game Mechanics

When Gareth Damian Martin began work on the game, he wasn't planning a major revolution. Citizen Sleeper 2: Starward Vector still borrows heavily from paper RPGs. The gameplay is divided into cycles, and our main task is to ensure that the main character survives as long as possible to have a chance of escaping the employer who is looking for him.

At the beginning of each cycle, we roll the dice and assign numbers to selected actions or events (so-called dice placement). There are no classical tasks either, which the Creator has replaced with drives. They are responsible for the development of the hero's skills, such as Engineering and Stamina.

This does not mean that the second Citizen Sleeper is "just" a new story in an almost identical game. This time, the protagonist doesn't live on a dilapidated space station, but has his own ship that needs repairs and a crew. All this requires resources, which we can only obtain by taking on often risky missions, which can also bring the hero's former employer to our aid.
