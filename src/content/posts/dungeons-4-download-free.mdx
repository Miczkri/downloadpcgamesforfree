## Game Mechanics

Dungeons 4 takes a bird's eye view of the action. As before, the gameplay is based on two pillars. The first is the construction of dungeons, which are the home and base of operations for our minions. The dungeons must not only be large and comfortable, but also properly secured. After all, the heart of a dungeon is a greedy morsel for both humans and dwarves, who often organize expeditions there.

The second pillar of the game is skirmishes on the surface, to which we send troops recruited in the underground. The core of our army is made up of undead, demons, and the horde; the importance of the goblins has also been increased. We also use Thalya's various abilities in dynamic battles. In addition to ranked enemy units, we will also encounter bosses (such as the unicorn) on the battlefield. With successive victories, we accumulate evil, which we use to gradually transform the green forests and glades into an awe-inspiring empire.

The developers have tried to make everything bigger than before. The surface world and the underworld are much larger than in Dungeons 3, we can send huge armies of different units into battle, and the range of options for controlling the dark creatures and punishing them for their misdeeds is also wider than before.
