## Plot of the game

The action of Norland takes place in a medieval town ruled by a noble family. This title does not have a predetermined top-down plot. Rather, it is a generator of elaborate stories in which each interaction with unique characters has an impact on the further development of events.

## Game mechanics

In this game, we take control of a noble family that rules over a city inhabited by representatives of different classes: peasants, soldiers, slaves, criminals. Each citizen has their own needs and behaviours, depending on their social status and individual character traits. Similarly, members of the aristocracy develop complex relationships with each other over time (making friends, falling in love, supporting each other, but also slandering, insulting and, in drastic cases, killing). Each individual has unique personality traits, but also family traits that can be passed on to future generations. Each individual also has a different background, belief system, level of sexual drive, etc.

The aim of the game is to develop the city and keep our citizens happy. We can achieve this by paying high salaries, punishing criminals, increasing the population, building and improving production buildings, controlling prices on the internal market, trading with visiting merchants and solving any emergencies. It is also important to ensure the safety of both the noble family and the inhabitants in the event of potential bloody riots or natural disasters.

## Dominate your opponents

There are many ways to maintain control of a city and increase your influence in the world. Not only can we plot political intrigues with other rulers or religious leaders, but we can also blackmail or order assassinations. We also have the ability to grow our armies and build defences, and it's up to us whether we use them to fight other kingdoms or choose a more peaceful path.
