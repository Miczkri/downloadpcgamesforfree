## The garden is seen from a microcosmic perspective

Grounded 2 takes us to Brookhollow Park, a vast area full of the atmosphere of the early 1990s. The starting zone is already larger than the entire previous backyard, and new biomes, such as deserted ice cream stands and abandoned anthills, introduce diverse environments with unique conditions. This inevitably stimulates a sense of nostalgia, as well as curiosity and a desire to discover what lies behind the old signs and mysterious traces.

The survival mechanics remain true to their roots, incorporating crafting, base building, combat and cooperation, but these are now complemented by brand new elements. Buggies, which we can tame and ride, radically change the way we move around the world, providing dynamic and exciting journeys. New monsters, such as cockroaches lurking in the shadows, can block attacks, making each encounter more challenging and rewarding.

## Is this Survival 2.0?

Obsidian does not treat Grounded 2 as a simple sequel, but as an evolution of the experience. Combat 2.0 introduces dodges, smarter enemy behaviour and more varied combat patterns. All of these features make encounters more exciting and strategic. Whether you're playing alone or as part of a four-person team, each battle requires attention, planning and the ability to react quickly to your opponent's tactics.

There will also be a character development system based on archetypes that allows you to customise your playstyle, whether by accessing new building options or discovering more effective ways to defeat larvae and creatures. The skill upgrades are promising, as evidenced by the community's keen interest.

## Game Preview – Development with Players

Grounded showed in its first instalment that Obsidian knows how to listen to the community. Grounded 2 follows this formula: Game Preview launches on 29 July 2025 and will be available on Xbox Series X, Xbox Series S and PC (Steam and Microsoft Store), as well as through Game Pass. Although a PlayStation 5 version has not yet been announced, the first instalment was released on that console and it is likely that the second instalment will also be released on Sony's platform in the future. There is also a strong possibility that the game will appear on the Nintendo Switch 2.

This stage of production is distinguished by a public roadmap, regular updates, and responsiveness to player feedback. Obsidian has announced that the initial stage will feature extensive content, including an elaborate storyline, systems and game structure, all of which are based on collective player decisions and suggestions.
