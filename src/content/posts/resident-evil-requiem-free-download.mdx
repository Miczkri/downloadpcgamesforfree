## FPP or TPP? The choice is yours!

From the very first encounter, Resident Evil Requiem shows that Capcom can still surprise fans of the series. With a new protagonist, dark and claustrophobic locations, a brand new camera system and a pursuer to rival Lady Dimitrescu and Mr X, this instalment is one of the strongest in the franchise. At the trade fair in Cologne, we had the opportunity to try out the game and see if Capcom delivers the horror we've come to expect.

Right from the outset, Capcom gives players the option to play in either first-person or third-person mode. Previous instalments have only experimented with one perspective at a time – Resident Evil 7 and Village were FPP, while the classic remakes introduced TPP. Now, players can decide for themselves. Moreover, the differences are not limited to the camera view. Some scenes and animations, such as Grace's fall to the ground, are only available in third-person mode, encouraging players to approach the game differently.

## A flesh-and-blood heroine

In Requiem, we play as Grace Ashcroft, who is imprisoned in the gloomy Rhodes Hill Chronic Care Center. The task seems simple enough: escape by searching for items, solving puzzles, and avoiding death at the hands of a new monster.

The corridors are extremely claustrophobic and dark, and all kinds of disturbing sounds come from everywhere. I could feel how terrified Grace was at all times, which made me even more anxious.

Fortunately, Grace is not one of those characters who constantly talk to themselves. Her body language and cries conveyed justified nervousness. After assessing the situation, exploring for a few minutes, and reading notes that didn't clarify our location, I knew my main goal was to find a fuse to open the grate blocking the escape route.

The animations are also noteworthy. Capcom created unique movements for the heroine depending on the selected perspective. In TPP, Grace's character moves with a full set of animations typical of horror "final girls," enhancing the feeling of participating in a cinematic nightmare. In FPP, the focus is on immersion and the limitations of seeing through the character's eyes.

## Light saves lives

While exploring, I found a lighter that illuminated a dark corridor. I had previously avoided entering it because it was too dark. That's how I reached the door from which an "infected," as Grace called it, jumped out, followed by a giant monster that grabbed the corpse and bit off its head.

Fans of the Resident Evil series certainly associate Lady Dimitrescu with Resident Evil Village, and Mr. X with "two." The ninth installment introduces a similar opponent who frightens us with his mere presence. He relentlessly pursues and hunts us down. He is so terrifying that he is hard to describe.

He is so huge that his entire body takes up most of the hallway, leaving no room to slip past him. In the building, we find glass bottles that we can use to distract the monster. This works reasonably well, forcing us to carefully plan where to throw the bottle to give ourselves enough time to find what we need.

We can also find green plants that serve as medicine, as usual in this series. The giant monster is weak to light, so my main strategy for dealing with this three-meter problem that wouldn't leave me alone was to enter rooms where there was a source of beneficial photons. Sometimes it would disappear, only to reappear right in front of me.

## The graphics will blow you away!

Resident Evil Requiem uses the RE Engine, which has powered all of Capcom's major titles for several years, including RE7 and Street Fighter 6. This engine is renowned for its exceptional optimisation and high-quality visuals, and Requiem showcases its full potential.

The graphics are stunning. The claustrophobic interiors of Rhodes Hill are filled with realistic details and reflections, as well as dynamic lighting that plays a key role in gameplay. Light is not just a visual effect here; it's an integral mechanic that affects survival.

Requiem runs smoothly without any visible issues. The game maintains 60 frames per second, even during scenes with dynamic lighting and particle effects. Capcom has also emphasized full ray tracing support and extensive graphics configuration options on PC, suggesting that the game will look impressive regardless of the platform.

## Be sure to put on your headphones!

Sound plays a huge role in building the atmosphere. The echo of footsteps in the hallway, the slam of closing doors, and Grace's muffled breaths effectively heighten the tension. The use of 3D audio allows you to precisely locate threats. All of this is complemented by a control and physics system that makes using objects — such as bottles to distract opponents — intuitive and a natural fit for the cramped spaces of the hospital.
