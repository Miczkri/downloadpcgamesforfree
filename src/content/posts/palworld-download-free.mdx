## The plot of the game

Palworld takes us on a journey to a fictional world where humans coexist with Pokemon-like creatures known as Pale (Pals). As the game progresses, we will travel around this picturesque land, capturing new creatures and using their unique powers and abilities in a variety of ways. It's up to us to treat them humanely or, on the contrary, to exploit them to the limit and then trade them in for healthier, stronger specimens.

## Game mechanics

In Palworld we see the action from a third-person perspective (TPP). We have at our disposal a vast world made up of different locations. We can find dense forests and beautiful plains, as well as huge canyons and snow-covered wastelands.

As we explore, we capture creatures that vary in size, appearance and ability, and use them in a variety of ways. Some are great for building, others are perfect for farming, resource gathering or factories, and still others we can use to move around the world faster (using them as mounts) or to defend ourselves from lurking dangers. With the help of Pali, we build camps, in which more and more sophisticated structures appear over time (such as pyramids or rockets), we farm the land (individual creatures perform specific actions on our plot, such as sowing, watering and harvesting) and take care of our headquarters (which someone needs to clean), as well as building powerful factories and running automated production chains that allow us to produce a wide variety of goods.

## Build an army

We can multiply our creatures over time and even crossbreed them to create unique representatives of new species. We may also come across rare, endangered creatures on our travels. Catching them is forbidden, but the game can be worth it, as selling them can make you rich quickly. Just remember not to get caught while hunting. As for how to deal with your Pales - creatures that are exploited to the limit will work more efficiently, but will not live too long, while creatures that are treated well will enjoy a longer life, although we will have to wait longer for the effects of their work.

At the same time, Palworld is teeming with threats - from violent atmospheric phenomena to hostile humans and creatures fighting alongside them. Against the latter, we can defend ourselves with the help of the killing tools we make in our factories. When all else fails, we have no choice but to flee, throwing our creatures at the mercy of our enemies to slow down the hunt and save our own skins.
