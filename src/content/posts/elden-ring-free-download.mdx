## The game's plot

Following the pattern of games in the series commonly referred to as Soulsborne - which includes Demon's Souls, the Dark Souls trilogy and Bloodborne - Elden Ring's main storyline is lightly sketched out, and the player must progress through the stages of the adventure to piece together bits of information that will eventually form a coherent whole.

The production takes place in a land known as The Lands Between, which is divided into six kingdoms. Each is ruled by one of the demigods that we must defeat in order to reclaim the fragments of the Elden Circle. It was the shattering of this mysterious object that triggered the cataclysm that brought the Lands Between to the brink of collapse. The main character, one of the Damaged, returned home from exile to try to regain the once-lost favour of the Golden Earthtree, prove his worth and become the new Elden Lord.

To create the story of the Elden Ring world, FromSoftware enlisted the help of George R.R. Martin - an author best known for the book-length saga A Song of Ice and Fire, on which HBO's Game of Thrones is based. His work was modified to some extent by the game's authors, resulting in a complex, dark fantasy world.

At the same time, it only affects the initial statistics and equipment of our rogue. Indeed, Elden Ring places great emphasis on role-playing elements, offering players freedom in character development, which, combined with a wide arsenal of weapons and spells, makes it possible to adapt the appropriate tactics to each situation.

One of the main attractions of the title is the complex arcade combat system. Clashes with enemies take place in a dynamic manner and each attack is very precise. FromSoftware is famous for creating challenging productions, and Elden Ring is no exception - each duel should be treated with caution, while the variety of enemies requires us to constantly invent new tactics.
