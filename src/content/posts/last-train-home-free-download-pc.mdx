## The plot of the game

Last Train Home takes us to the realities of the Russian Civil War - an armed conflict that took place in 1917-1922, in which the armies of the Reds (Communists or Bolsheviks) and the Whites (all organizations striving to restore Tsarism) fought for power in the country. The player controls the actions of the soldiers of the Czechoslovak Legion - a real military formation formed during the First World War from prisoners and deserters of Czech and Slovak nationality who had previously belonged to the Austro-Hungarian army.

The unit was caught on the wrong side of the conflict. Our task is to help them return to their homeland on the last train of the title. The tank depot serves both as a means of transportation for the soldiers through the wild, desolate, hostile terrain of Siberia - with only a little protection from the pervasive cold - and as a weapon to fight the two armies that are tearing the country apart.

## Game mechanics

Evacuating Russia requires many stops - sometimes you run out of food or medicine, sometimes the train breaks down and you need to find a way to repair it. This is where Last Train Home becomes an RTS. The game offers more than a dozen challenging missions in which we - choosing our own tactics - have to achieve a series of predetermined goals.

The ease or difficulty of achieving them depends on a number of factors. One of them is the combat potential of our soldiers - if we send them out hungry, they will be less effective than if they go out with a full stomach. The tactics we choose are also important. Some tasks can be done "quietly", reducing the likelihood of Legion members dying in battle. However, not everyone is cut out for this, so you must consider your charges' dispositions.

## Your decisions matter

The deaths of misfits or other morally ambiguous decisions we make will manifest themselves in various statuses that affect gameplay. For example, we can decide the fate of several innocent civilians by taking them with us or not. In the first case, we will have more "mouths to feed". In the second case, our charges will develop remorse, which will have a negative effect on their morale.

The title train acts as a sort of base camp. Between missions, we can repair and modify it, assigning soldiers to specific tasks - such as caring for the wounded - to ensure the best possible living conditions. This is also where we improve our troops - warriors can be decorated, and by participating in training they gain new skills (e.g. herbalism or hunting) - and equip them with better equipment acquired during missions.

At each stage of this strange odyssey, we learn the stories of the soldiers under command. Their experiences also affect the gameplay - for example, projecting a tendency to depression or how they evaluate our decisions. However, Ashborne Games Studio has taken care to keep such "effects" to a minimum, so that players who want to complete Last Train Home solely for the story will not be discouraged by too high a difficulty level.
