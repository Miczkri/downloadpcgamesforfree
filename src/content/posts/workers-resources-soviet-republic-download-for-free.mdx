## The plot of the game

In Workers & Resources: Soviet Republic, the player assumes the role of an administrator whose task it is to develop the city. An important feature of the game is its freedom. The game does not impose specific goals: the direction in which the state will be led is left to the discretion of the player.

## Game mechanics

The game mechanics do not differ much from standard representatives of the genre. The player gets a certain territory on which he can develop his own state. From now on he has to develop it in such a way that it is able to earn for itself. To do this, the player is given a number of different types of buildings to choose from in order to find a compromise between the needs of the population and the finances of the republic. The construction of a certain building requires the accumulation of a certain amount of certain resources.

Of course, the entire industry will not work on its own. Each factory must be staffed by a certain number of workers with the appropriate skills. Therefore, workers must have access to education in order to acquire the appropriate skills for a given position. That's why schools must be located near residential areas to guarantee this. In addition, workers have their own needs that grow over time.

## The demands of society increase

This means that while the average person will initially be satisfied with easy access to food, they will later begin to demand goods such as alcohol or electronics. Whether their needs are adequately met affects their productivity at work. Children are also an important aspect of the economy. On the one hand, they help maintain an adequate number of citizens; on the other hand, if a child is under six years old and there is no kindergarten in the area of the residential neighborhood, its parents will refuse to work.

## Imports and Exports

Transportation also plays an important role in the Republic. All goods must be transported, whether to the nearest factory or to a specific warehouse. The same goes for workers. If they live too far from their place of work, they will want to use public transportation. The creation of an adequate transportation network is therefore necessary for the smooth functioning of the city.

Products from factories need to be monetized. Prices change dynamically, depending on production costs and other factors. It is possible to sell goods in two separate markets: Eastern and Western. If you sell your goods to countries behind the Iron Curtain, you will receive payment in dollars, which you will have to convert into rubles. So it depends on the exchange rate of the currency whether the player can actually make money.

## Technical Questions

Workers & Resources: Soviet Republic places great emphasis on realism. All buildings are inspired by real buildings from the Soviet era. In addition, the entire visuals are kept in dark colors, which enhances the impression of being in one of the Soviet republics.
