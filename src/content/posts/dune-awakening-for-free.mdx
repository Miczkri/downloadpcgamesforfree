In Dune: Awakening, whose plot is an alternative version of the events from Frank Herbert's books and their film adaptations, you play as a convict who has been sent to Arrakis. Your mission: investigate mysterious disappearances and... survive. There is no time for long introductions or peaceful beginnings. From the very first minute, you will be thrown into the sand and forced to deal with basic problems such as thirst and lack of shelter, not to mention the constant threat of sandworms.

## Surviving in the desert

The biggest novelty compared to classic MMORPGs is the strong emphasis on survival. As well as typical crafting and exploration, you will also have to take care of your character's condition. You must keep track of your thirst meter, avoid the sun, seek shelter during sandstorms and repair your suit. Sound familiar? Yes, it does. The developers were inspired by Valheim and other survival RPGs, but they also incorporated numerous elements characteristic of the Dune universe.

First and foremost, there are giant worms that react to vibrations. The developers warn that if you run recklessly across the open sand, you won't survive for long. Every step could bring the wrath of nature down upon you. You can't win against a worm's jaws.

## Build, hunt and survive!

Dune: Awakening features both combat and strategy. Players can build their own shelters and entire fortresses. They can also create guilds, trade and wage wars over spice. Harvesters will appear that you will need to place in the right locations and defend from other players and computer-controlled factions.

Interestingly, the landscape of Arrakis will change dynamically. Sandstorms will shift dunes, revealing new areas and obscuring old ones. This means that the game map will never be static, so reaching hidden locations first will give you a significant advantage.

## You will need equipment, spice and... hallucinations?

Filtraki, or water-filtering suits, are a key element of gameplay. They can be upgraded to help you survive harsh conditions more efficiently. As with weapons and other equipment, your success will depend on what you manage to create or find. As well as classic crafting, there will be a spice system — collecting and using spice will affect your character's stats and your perception. Spice can give you new abilities, but it can also distort your perception of reality.

The developers have announced that you will be able to 'consume spices to overcome the limitations of the senses and gain new powers'. Psychedelic visions will not only be an addition, but also part of an extensive character development system.
