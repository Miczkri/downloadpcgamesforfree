## The Wild West is coming to an end

The end of the 19th century was a time of intense social change in the Wild West. The wheels of history are slowly grinding down cowboys roaming the prairies and indigenous peoples are also losing their easy lives, being ruthlessly pushed to the margins of society and deprived of dignity. They are unable to find peace, even in the reservations. Turbulent times lie ahead: the idyllic scenes reminiscent of Paul Kane's paintings will give way to an industrial landscape. Horse-drawn transport will be replaced by four-wheeled machines. Above all, the hand of justice will punish more effectively, causing outlaws to think twice before disturbing the peace of law-abiding citizens.

Arthur Morgan, a member of the infamous Van der Linde gang, has no time for nostalgia. When we first encounter him, he is forced to make a desperate escape: the gang's hideout in Blackwater has been 'burned', and the enraged tycoon Leviticus Cornwall is sparing no time or expense to hunt down the criminals, dead or alive. The aggressive actions of the Pinkerton Agency, which Cornwall is funding, keep the gang on the move as they try to find peace in a world that has condemned them to extinction. The infamous Dutch van der Linde – a charming thug with a heavy hand and his own moral code – leads the gang straight into the arms of destiny.

## A first-person Western

Players who fell in love with the original Red Dead Redemption will be captivated by the magic of the Wild West from the very first minutes. We find ourselves in a blizzard reminiscent of Tarantino's The Hateful Eight. Snow blinds our eyes, horses struggle to walk through treacherous drifts and a clash with a rival gang looms on the horizon. As we proceed at a leisurely pace, we get to know the first members of our community and are introduced to the group's hierarchy.

These relationships will form the basis of Arthur's entire evolution. They will have a real impact on the fate of Dutch's right-hand man. Whether we follow the path of righteousness or turn Morgan into the biggest bastard West Elizabeth has ever seen is up to us. The number of activities available from the outset is overwhelming. As part of the main missions, we will fight local warlords, collect debts and rob banks, trains and private farms. But don't worry — this is only a fraction of what awaits us.

The world of Red Dead Redemption 2 boasts the most dazzling and vast scenery I have ever seen in a video game. Until recently, I thought the latest Assassin's Creed was beautiful and captivating, but the ancient Greek landscapes pale in comparison to what Rockstar has created here. To the east lie misty, damp swamps where alligators eye us greedily. The north is a frosty land where winter seems endless, icicles hang from Arthur's nose and a lack of warm clothing can lead to rapid hypothermia. The west comprises the dry, vast prairies that we know from John Marston's adventures. Their vastness evokes both anxiety and nostalgia. Between these areas, however, are the uncultivated remnants of former forests, which provide shelter for a variety of fauna.

## The best sandbox in history!

Each of these places affects Morgan differently. Sand bites into the nostrils of our mount, dirt clings to the bloodstained shirt of Arthur, and weapons freeze and jam in the mountains, swept by blizzards. Every detail has been crafted with passion, care and commitment. When gathering herbs to make healing potions, we must be on our guard so as not to fall prey to predators. While the coyotes we encounter are more likely to flee than attack head-on, confronting an enraged grizzly bear could quickly result in the main character's death. But what if we want to hunt big game ourselves? Arthur employs a variety of techniques to help him track animals effectively. These include scent masking, carnivore lures and appropriate ammunition. Each of these makes it easier to catch and skin furry animals quickly. We then exchange the skins and trophies we have obtained with a trapper for protective clothing.

The dynamics of this world deserve special praise. When travelling through the wilderness, we cannot be sure of anything. Just because we are attacked by bandits in one place does not mean that the same thing will happen to our friends. Encounters with strangers in need of help are unpredictable. This world lives and functions independently of our actions and decisions. The old man whose wound we sucked the poison from may appear in a nearby town or greet us on the trail in a completely different part of the country. Whether we help the poor man begging by the roadside is up to us. One time, he may be just a homeless person; another time, he may be a lookout for bandits lurking in the bushes. Women are kidnapped, wolves chase careless travellers and the horse we scare may fatally wound its owner and escape into the wilderness. However, there is no 'abuse' of the mechanics here; the world is consistent and believable. During the almost 60 hours that I spent playing the game, there was never a time when I had to take someone's word for it.

## The side missions are small works of art

The above activities pale in comparison to the side quests from strangers encountered along the way — these missions often span several chapters. For example, we can help a paleontologist looking for hidden dinosaur bones. We can become an assistant to a mad scientist who wants to bring his own Frankenstein monster to life. Alternatively, we can hand a wanted fugitive over to the authorities and explain to him what cows struck by lightning look like and what awaits him in the electric chair. This is just the tip of the iceberg.

A devoted companion will assist the hero in their travels. This devoted companion's level of cooperation varies depending on the breed. The relationship with the mount is not easy, and it's clear that the developers wanted to fully reflect what's going on in the horse's mind. Sounds silly? Perhaps, but in practice it works perfectly: a newly acquired horse will not listen to Arthur, will be skittish, and may even kick him out of pure malice. However, taking care of your mount, feeding it oatmeal cookies and grooming it regularly builds trust and makes it more responsive to your commands. A horse's trust greatly affects its endurance and speed, as well as its behaviour in crisis situations – a well-trained horse will come running faster when called and will even trample its opponents without hesitation. This is not just a gimmick; it's Arthur's true sidekick. Without him, the grumpy gunslinger wouldn't be able to achieve much. We become accustomed to his presence and care about his fate. Seeing a stunned animal unable to get up is an emotional rollercoaster. The same goes for a failed encounter with horse thieves, after which you usually have to find a new mount.

## The multidimensional Arthur Morgan

Arthur himself remains an enigma. His initial devotion to Dutch, his taciturn nature, and his willingness to carry out orders without hesitation can make him seem unfriendly, grumpy, and even boring. Of course, this is merely an illusion – I won't reveal any more in case I spoil the fun for anyone, but suffice it to say that nothing here is as it seems. Red Dead Redemption 2 features strong, vivid characters whose actions can surprise, disappoint or amaze us. I watched with great satisfaction as Sadie Adler evolved – a farmer's wife rescued by a gang who was dissatisfied with her role as camp cook, and whose priority was to regain control of her destiny.

Long before the release of Red Dead Redemption 2 (and after the first gameplay footage was presented), the internet exploded and gamers went crazy. The game of the generation? A masterpiece? A magnum opus? I wouldn't dare use such bold words – it's certainly not Rockstar's last hit. However, it has been a long time since I encountered a game that triggered such a wide range of emotions. From joy and silly laughter to crying, anger and bitterness. Red Dead Redemption 2 consists of six chapters, a fantastic epilogue and countless activities that can easily fill 80–100 hours of gameplay. But above all, it features tens of thousands of lines of dialogue and countless poetic moments that come together to form a thoughtful, deep storyline. It's hard to list all the attractions that Rockstar has given us in its latest production. Nevertheless, it's safe to say that this is one of the most complete and wonderful games I have ever played.
